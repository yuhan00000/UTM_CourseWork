Human Computer Interaction (HCI) introduced the principles of designing technology that is effective, accessible, and user-friendly. The course emphasized the importance of understanding user needs, usability principles, interface design, and evaluation techniques when developing digital systems.

Through design activities, prototyping exercises, and group projects, valuable experience was gained in creating solutions that prioritize the user experience rather than focusing solely on technical functionality. The process of identifying user requirements, designing interfaces, and evaluating usability highlighted the importance of empathy and user-centered thinking in system development.

One of the most valuable lessons learned was that a technically functional system does not necessarily provide a positive user experience. Small design decisions such as navigation structure, visual layout, consistency, and accessibility can significantly influence user satisfaction. Conducting usability evaluations and receiving feedback from potential users demonstrated how iterative improvements contribute to better system design.

The course also strengthened teamwork, communication, and presentation skills through collaborative project work. These experiences provided practical exposure to the design process commonly used in professional software development environments.

Overall, Human Computer Interaction broadened the understanding of software development by emphasizing the human aspect of technology and the importance of designing systems that are intuitive, efficient, and enjoyable to use.

### Future Improvement

Future development could focus on learning advanced UI/UX design tools and conducting more comprehensive user testing to further improve the ability to design user-centered applications.
