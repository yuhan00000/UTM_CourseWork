System Analysis and Design provided valuable exposure to the structured processes involved in developing information systems. The course introduced key concepts such as requirements analysis, process modeling, system planning, feasibility studies, use case development, and system design methodologies.

Through project-based activities and case studies, practical experience was gained in analyzing business problems and translating user requirements into system solutions. The course emphasized the importance of understanding stakeholder needs before beginning system development, highlighting that successful systems depend on both technical implementation and effective requirements gathering.

One of the most important lessons learned was the significance of proper planning and documentation. Creating diagrams, models, and system specifications demonstrated how structured design approaches help reduce development risks and improve communication among stakeholders. The course also strengthened critical thinking skills by requiring evaluation of different solution approaches based on business requirements.

Collaborative project work further enhanced teamwork, communication, and project management abilities. These experiences reflected real-world software development practices where successful outcomes depend on coordination between technical teams and business users.

Overall, the course provided a comprehensive understanding of the software development lifecycle and established a strong foundation for future courses such as Software Engineering, Enterprise System Design and Modelling, Application Development, and System Development Technology.

### Future Improvement

Future learning could focus on applying modern agile methodologies and industry-standard modeling tools to gain deeper exposure to contemporary system development practices.
