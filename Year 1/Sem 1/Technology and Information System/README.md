Technology and Information System (TIS) served as an important introduction to the information technology industry and the role of technology in modern organizations. Unlike programming-oriented courses, this subject provided broader exposure to emerging technologies, industry practices, digital transformation, and the various career pathways available within the computing field.

Throughout the semester, participation in industry talks, industry visits, technical report writing, and presentations provided valuable opportunities to connect academic learning with real-world applications. Exposure to professionals from different sectors offered insights into current industry trends, workplace expectations, and the technologies used by organizations to solve business challenges. These experiences helped develop a clearer understanding of how computing knowledge is applied beyond the classroom.

One of the most significant learning outcomes from this course was the realization that technical skills alone are not sufficient for success in the technology industry. Communication, teamwork, problem-solving, adaptability, and lifelong learning were repeatedly emphasized by industry practitioners. Working on group assignments also strengthened collaboration skills, project coordination, and the ability to communicate ideas effectively through both written reports and presentations.

The course encouraged greater awareness of current technological developments and inspired further exploration of areas such as data engineering, cloud computing, artificial intelligence, and business intelligence. The industry engagement activities were particularly beneficial in helping bridge the gap between academic studies and professional practice.

Overall, Technology and Information System provided a valuable foundation for understanding the broader technology ecosystem and played an important role in shaping future academic and career interests within the computing field.
