Digital Logic provided a strong foundation in understanding how computer systems operate at the hardware level. Through topics such as number systems, Boolean algebra, logic gates, combinational circuits, and sequential circuits, this course introduced the fundamental concepts behind modern computing devices.

Throughout the semester, practical exercises and problem-solving activities helped strengthen logical thinking and analytical skills. Designing and simplifying logic circuits required careful analysis and attention to detail, which improved the ability to approach technical problems systematically. The course also highlighted how complex digital systems are built from simple logical components, allowing a deeper appreciation of computer architecture and hardware design.

One of the main challenges encountered was understanding the relationship between theoretical concepts and actual circuit implementation. Initially, translating truth tables and Boolean expressions into circuit diagrams required considerable practice. However, continuous exposure to exercises and discussions gradually improved confidence in applying these concepts.

The knowledge gained from Digital Logic serves as an important foundation for subsequent subjects such as Computer Organization and Architecture, Embedded Systems, and other hardware-related courses. Overall, this course successfully developed logical reasoning skills and provided valuable insight into the fundamental building blocks of computing systems.

### Future Improvement

Future learning could involve exploring hardware simulation tools and field-programmable gate arrays (FPGAs) to gain more hands-on experience in digital system design and implementation.
