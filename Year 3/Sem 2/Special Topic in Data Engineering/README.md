## Course Overview

Special Topic in Data Engineering provided practical exposure to modern data engineering technologies, cloud platforms, big data processing frameworks, and industry practices. Through industry talks, technical tutorials, project development, and independent learning activities, the course bridged the gap between academic knowledge and real-world data engineering environments.

## Industry Talk 1 – iZeno & Logicalis: From Data to Intelligence

The industry talk delivered by iZeno and Logicalis provided valuable insight into the realities of working with data in enterprise environments. One of the most impactful discussions focused on the gap between academic learning and industry practice. While university projects often involve relatively small and clean datasets, enterprise environments must handle massive volumes of messy, continuously changing data while maintaining reliability and scalability.

The case study involving the implementation of Snowflake Cortex LLM for an automated news intelligence platform demonstrated how modern organizations are integrating artificial intelligence into data workflows. Learning how automation reduced manual effort by approximately 90 percent highlighted the practical value of data engineering and AI technologies in solving business problems.

This session reinforced the importance of learning technologies such as cloud platforms, containerization, orchestration tools, and scalable data pipelines that are commonly used in industry.

## Industry Talk 2 – TM One

The TM One industry talk provided valuable exposure to enterprise-scale data architecture and the challenges faced by organizations undergoing digital transformation. The session highlighted that the complexity of enterprise data environments often stems not only from data volume but also from evolving business processes, legacy systems, and fragmented information sources.

The case study presented by Mr. Zaid Waqi Zulkifli demonstrated how enterprise organizations utilize Azure, Snowflake, and Databricks to build unified data platforms capable of supporting large-scale business operations. Understanding how architecture decisions influence scalability, governance, and business intelligence provided a clearer perspective on real-world data engineering practices.

This session strengthened understanding of enterprise data ecosystems and emphasized the importance of designing data solutions that align with business requirements and long-term organizational goals.

## Industry Visit – PPG Coatings (Malaysia) Sdn. Bhd.

The industry visit to PPG Coatings (Malaysia) Sdn. Bhd. provided valuable exposure to how technology and data are utilized within a large multinational organization. The visit offered an opportunity to observe how business operations, enterprise systems, and data-driven decision-making are integrated within a real industrial environment.

One of the most significant learning experiences was understanding how organizations manage Business-as-Usual (BAU) operations while simultaneously developing and improving new technological solutions. This provided a practical perspective on how companies balance operational stability with continuous innovation. Observing these processes helped bridge the gap between theoretical concepts learned in university and real-world business practices.

The session on SAP Business Technology Platform (SAP BTP) was particularly insightful. Learning how applications are developed, integrated, and maintained within an enterprise environment demonstrated the importance of system integration and digital transformation in modern organizations. Exposure to technologies such as Microsoft Azure and Power BI also highlighted how cloud platforms and analytics solutions support data management, reporting, and strategic decision-making.

A valuable aspect of the visit was the opportunity to interact directly with SAP CoE functional and technical leaders as well as the Data & Analytics CoE leader. These discussions provided deeper insight into industry expectations, workplace culture, technical responsibilities, and the skills required for future careers in technology and data-related fields. Conversations with senior interns further strengthened understanding of internship experiences and professional development pathways.

Most importantly, the visit provided a clearer understanding of how data engineering, cloud technologies, enterprise systems, and business intelligence solutions are applied in large-scale organizations. The experience reinforced the relevance of academic studies and provided motivation to continue developing both technical and professional competencies.

## Assignment 1 – Academic Writing

The academic writing assignment strengthened research, analytical thinking, and technical writing skills. Conducting literature reviews, evaluating sources, and presenting arguments in a structured manner improved the ability to communicate technical concepts professionally and support claims with credible evidence.

## Tutorial 1 - Microsoft Azure

The Azure tutorial provided practical experience with cloud-based data engineering technologies. Through hands-on activities involving Azure services, greater understanding was developed regarding cloud storage, data pipelines, analytics services, and scalable data architectures.

The tutorial demonstrated how cloud platforms simplify the deployment and management of modern data solutions while supporting scalability, reliability, and integration across services.

## Tutorial 2 - Apache Spark

The Apache Spark tutorial introduced distributed data processing concepts and provided practical exposure to one of the most widely used big data frameworks. Learning how Spark distributes workloads across multiple computing resources demonstrated the advantages of parallel processing when working with large datasets.

The tutorial also strengthened understanding of scalability, performance optimization, and data transformation techniques commonly used in modern data engineering workflows.

## Tutorial 3 - AI Algorithm

The AI-assisted data engineering tutorial explored how modern AI tools can support different stages of the data engineering lifecycle, including planning, development, documentation, and problem-solving. Through practical experimentation, valuable insights were gained regarding the strengths and limitations of AI-assisted development approaches.

The experience demonstrated how AI can improve productivity while still requiring human judgment, validation, and domain expertise to ensure solution quality and reliability.

## Tutorial 4 - Claude to Pipeline

The AI-Assisted Data Engineering project provided practical exposure to the emerging role of generative AI in software and data engineering workflows. The project explored how AI tools can support various stages of system development, including project planning, architecture design, code generation, documentation, testing, and implementation.

Throughout the project, AI was utilized as a collaborative assistant to accelerate development activities and improve productivity. The experience demonstrated that AI can significantly reduce the time required for repetitive tasks, generate implementation ideas, and assist with troubleshooting. However, the project also highlighted that human expertise remains essential for validating outputs, refining solutions, and ensuring technical correctness.

A particularly valuable lesson learned was the importance of prompt engineering and critical evaluation when working with AI-generated content. The quality of results depended heavily on the clarity of instructions provided to the AI system. Effective use of AI required iterative refinement, verification, and technical understanding rather than blind acceptance of generated outputs.

The project also provided insight into how AI-assisted development is transforming the software and data engineering landscape. Exposure to this workflow strengthened adaptability and highlighted the importance of learning how to collaborate effectively with AI technologies in future professional environments.

Overall, the project demonstrated the potential of AI as a productivity-enhancing tool while reinforcing the continued importance of human judgment, domain expertise, and problem-solving skills.

### Future Improvement

Future exploration could involve integrating AI into larger-scale data engineering workflows, evaluating different AI development platforms, and studying best practices for responsible and effective AI-assisted software development.

## Tutorial 5 - Article

The tutorial article assignment provided valuable experience in academic research, technical writing, and literature analysis. The task required the review of existing studies, identification of relevant concepts, and presentation of findings in a structured academic format. Through this process, students developed a deeper understanding of how research contributes to technological advancement and professional knowledge sharing.

One of the most important learning outcomes was the ability to evaluate information critically rather than simply collecting facts. Conducting literature reviews required comparing different sources, identifying key findings, and synthesizing information into a coherent discussion. This strengthened analytical thinking and improved the ability to communicate technical concepts using formal academic writing conventions.

The assignment also reinforced the importance of proper citation practices, academic integrity, and evidence-based arguments. These skills are essential for both academic research and professional documentation within the technology industry.

Overall, the tutorial article enhanced research capabilities and strengthened confidence in producing structured technical reports supported by credible sources.

## Industry Project – Inventory Risk Management Data Platform

The PPG Inventory Risk Management project represented the most comprehensive data engineering experience throughout the degree program. The project involved the design and implementation of a modern cloud-based data platform using Azure Data Lake Storage, Azure Databricks, Azure Synapse Analytics, and Power BI.

Working on the project provided practical exposure to data ingestion, transformation, storage, analytics, and visualization processes commonly found in enterprise environments. Developing Bronze, Silver, and Gold data layers reinforced understanding of data architecture principles and the importance of maintaining data quality throughout the pipeline.

The project also strengthened technical skills in cloud computing, ETL development, data modeling, analytics, and stakeholder communication. More importantly, it demonstrated how data engineering solutions can support business decision-making by transforming raw operational data into meaningful insights.

Among all academic projects completed throughout the degree program, this project provided the closest representation of a real-world data engineering implementation and significantly enhanced industry readiness.

## Overall Reflection

Special Topic in Data Engineering served as a culmination of knowledge acquired throughout the Data Engineering degree program. The combination of industry exposure, cloud technologies, big data frameworks, artificial intelligence tools, and enterprise project implementation provided a comprehensive understanding of modern data engineering practices.

The course strengthened both technical and professional competencies while providing valuable insight into industry expectations and emerging technology trends. Most importantly, it reinforced a strong interest in pursuing a career within the fields of data engineering, cloud analytics, and enterprise data platforms.
