Surfactant and Detergent Technology provided unique exposure to the scientific and industrial principles behind surfactants, detergents, and cleaning product formulations. The course introduced concepts related to chemical properties, production processes, product applications, sustainability considerations, and industry practices within the consumer goods sector.

Throughout the semester, students gained insight into how scientific knowledge is applied to solve practical challenges in manufacturing and product development. Learning about formulation processes, raw materials, and performance evaluation methods expanded understanding beyond traditional computing-related disciplines and highlighted the interdisciplinary nature of modern industries.

The course also emphasized the importance of sustainability, environmental considerations, and responsible production practices. These discussions encouraged a broader understanding of how technological and industrial decisions impact society and the environment.

Overall, the course provided valuable interdisciplinary knowledge and demonstrated how scientific, technological, and business considerations interact within industrial settings.
