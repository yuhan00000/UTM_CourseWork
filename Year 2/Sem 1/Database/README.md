Database introduced the fundamental principles of data management and organization, which are essential components of modern information systems. The course covered topics such as database design, entity-relationship modeling, normalization, relational databases, and SQL. These concepts provided a strong foundation for understanding how data is structured, stored, and accessed efficiently.

Throughout the semester, practical assignments and projects offered valuable experience in designing database solutions that satisfy business requirements. Developing entity-relationship diagrams and transforming them into normalized database structures strengthened analytical thinking and problem-solving skills. The course also emphasized the importance of maintaining data integrity, consistency, and efficiency within database systems.

One of the key challenges encountered was designing databases that balanced complexity and usability while maintaining proper normalization. Understanding relationships between entities and ensuring efficient database structures required careful planning and attention to detail. Through continuous practice and project development, greater confidence was gained in database modeling and query construction.

The knowledge acquired from this course became highly valuable in subsequent subjects such as Database Programming, Data Mining, Business Intelligence, and Data Engineering projects. Understanding how data is organized and managed serves as a critical foundation for data-driven systems and analytics.

### Future Improvement

Future development could involve exploring distributed databases, cloud-based database services, and database optimization techniques to gain deeper exposure to modern data management technologies.
